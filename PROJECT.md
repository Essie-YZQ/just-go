Just Go

Mission

Help people go from "Should we go?" to "Let's go." in under 30 minutes.

⸻

What is Just Go?

Just Go is an AI travel copilot designed for people who make spontaneous travel decisions.

Instead of spending hours researching hotels, restaurants, attractions, and transportation across multiple websites, users can simply describe their trip and receive a personalized travel plan in minutes.

⸻

The Problem

Planning a trip often takes longer than the trip itself.

People constantly switch between Google, Reddit, Booking, Google Maps, TikTok, Xiaohongshu, YouTube, and many other websites before making a decision.

The biggest problem isn't creating an itinerary.

The biggest problem is making a confident decision quickly.

⸻

Our Solution

Just Go reduces decision fatigue.

The goal is not to create the most detailed itinerary.

The goal is to help users confidently decide where to go, where to stay, what to eat, and what to do within minutes.

⸻

Core Differentiator

Recommendations should be personalized based on the traveler, not just the destination.

Different travelers trust different communities.

For example:

* A Chinese traveler may prefer recommendations inspired by Xiaohongshu.
* A local foodie may prefer Reddit or Eater.
* A museum lover may receive a completely different itinerary than a nightlife traveler.

The same city should generate different experiences for different people.

⸻

Product Philosophy

Every feature should answer one question:

Does this help users travel sooner with less effort?

If the answer is no, it probably doesn't belong in Just Go.

⸻

Trusted Sources

People trust different sources when planning a trip.

Instead of using the same recommendations for everyone, Just Go should personalize recommendations based on the information sources each traveler trusts.

Examples include:

* Xiaohongshu
* Reddit
* Google Reviews
* Eater
* Michelin Guide
* YouTube
* Local blogs

The same destination should generate different recommendations depending on the traveler's trusted sources.

⸻

Collaboration Guidelines

This project is developed collaboratively between the human (product owner) and Claude Code (software engineer).

Communication rules:

* All code, filenames, comments, variables, functions, classes, and technical implementation should remain in English.
* All explanations, discussions, reasoning, implementation plans, architecture decisions, and progress updates should be written in Simplified Chinese.
* When explaining technical concepts, keep all important technical keywords in English. For example: React, Component, API, State, Hook, Tailwind CSS, TypeScript, Next.js, localStorage, etc.
* Explain the overall logic in Chinese while preserving technical terminology in English.
* Assume the product owner has a technical background but is still learning modern AI application development.
* When making important architectural or product decisions, briefly explain the reasoning instead of only providing the result.
* Unless explicitly requested, do not over-explain basic programming concepts.
* When introducing a new AI concept or technology for the first time in this project, briefly explain what it is and why it is being used before implementing it.

This communication style should be followed throughout the entire project.
